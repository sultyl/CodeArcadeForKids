VOLUNTEERING FOR ROLES

  • Adesanmi Bolaji
  • June 20, 2022
  • 12 Comments

I remember the early days of my journey, a couple of years ago, when I embarked on a path with a vision of something bigger. Despite not having all the knowledge at the time, I knew there was a greater purpose ahead. It was during this phase that I discovered the significance of volunteering and how it shaped my personal and professional growth.

Right after my fellowship with #YALI (Young African Leaders Initiative), I took the initiative to partner with an organization that provided Ed-tech services to schools, taking on the role of an instructor. The position demanded technical expertise, and while I was transitioning from a different background, I embraced the challenge wholeheartedly.

Volunteering in this role allowed me to immerse myself in the world of education technology and gain practical experience. It provided a platform for me to refine my skills, connect with educators, and contribute to enhancing the learning experiences of students. The journey was not always easy, but I held on to a quote that had a profound impact on my determination and resilience.

The quote became a guiding principle in my life, especially during times of adversity. There was a period when I faced academic struggles, failing some courses and earning poor grades. It was in the midst of this discouragement that someone shared the quote with me. Although I didn't fully comprehend its meaning at the time, a few days later, the conversation replayed in my mind, and everything changed from that point forward.

“When we make progress quickly, it feeds our emotions. Then, when there’s a period of waiting or we hit a plateau, we find out how committed we really are and whether we’re going to see things through to the finish or quit.” – Joyce Meyer

From that moment on, I made a commitment to myself: in all that I do, I would persevere and ensure I finish whatever I have started. This mindset fueled my drive to overcome challenges, push past setbacks, and continue moving forward on my journey.

Volunteering played a crucial role in instilling this mindset within me. It provided me with opportunities to contribute, learn, and grow, even in areas where I initially felt inadequate. Through volunteering, I gained a deeper understanding of the value of dedication, determination, and the impact of serving others.

“Don’t count the days, make the days count.” – Muhammad Ali

Volunteering is not only about offering your time and skills; it is also a transformative experience that shapes character, builds resilience, and fosters personal and professional growth. Here are a few key benefits of volunteering:

Skill Development:

Volunteering allows you to acquire and enhance a wide range of skills, from technical expertise to communication, leadership, problem-solving, and teamwork.




Networking and Connections:

Volunteering connects you with like-minded individuals, professionals, and organizations in your field of interest, opening doors to valuable connections and opportunities.

Personal Growth and Confidence:

Volunteering challenges you to step out of your comfort zone, fostering personal growth, self-awareness, and the confidence to tackle new challenges.

Making a Difference:

By contributing your time and skills, you have the power to make a positive impact in the lives of others and within your community.

Career Advancement:

Volunteering can provide valuable experiences and references that strengthen your resume and set you apart in the job market.
